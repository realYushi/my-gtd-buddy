# GTD Scenarios & Recipes

Practical "recipes" for common productivity situations using the Claude Code GTD skill.

---

## 1. The "Post-Vacation" Reset
**Situation:** You just got back, Inbox has 50+ items, and everything feels overwhelming.

**Recipe:**
1. **Trigger:** `system is a mess` or `/gtd` (Health check will warn "Critical")
2. **Action:** Select **Recovery Mode**
   - **Option 1 (Bankruptcy):** Move all items older than 14 days to Someday.
   - **Option 2 (Inbox Blitz):** Rapid-fire "keep/delete" decisions only (no organizing).
3. **Finish:** Ask `focus on 3 things` to ignore the noise and pick just 3 key tasks to start momentum.

**Why it works:** Removes the guilt of a backlog and focuses on forward motion.

---

## 2. The "5-Minute Gap" Filler
**Situation:** A meeting ended early, or you're waiting for a call to start. You have ~10 minutes.

**Recipe:**
1. **Trigger:** `I have 10 mins` or `quick win`
2. **System Response:** Filters for tasks tagged `#quick`, `#5min`, or `#10min`.
3. **Action:** Knock out 2-3 small admin tasks (emails, quick calls) without deep context switching.

**Why it works:** Utilizes "dead time" for shallow work, saving deep work blocks for big tasks.

---

## 3. The "Friday Afternoon" Clear-down
**Situation:** Brain is fried, energy is low, but you want a clean slate for the weekend.

**Recipe:**
1. **Trigger:** `weekly review` (even if it's not Sunday)
2. **Steps:**
   - **Wins:** Review completed items for a dopamine hit.
   - **Waiting:** Check "Waiting For" list. Send quick "Any update?" emails now so replies come Monday.
   - **Mind Sweep:** Dump every nagging thought into the Inbox.
3. **Finish:** Close laptop knowing nothing is slipping through the cracks.

**Why it works:** Closes "open loops" that cause low-level anxiety during time off.

---

## 4. The "Project Breakdown"
**Situation:** You captured "Plan Conference" (a huge task) in your Inbox.

**Recipe:**
1. **Trigger:** `process inbox`
2. **Interaction:**
   - **System:** "1/5: 'Plan Conference'. Sounds like a project. What's ONE next action?"
   - **You:** "Email potential speakers"
   - **System:** Moves "Plan Conference" to **Projects** list. Adds "Email potential speakers" to **Next Actions**.

**Why it works:** Prevents "stuck" projects by forcing you to define the immediate next physical action.

---

## 5. The "Deep Work" Dive
**Situation:** You have a clear 2-hour block and high energy.

**Recipe:**
1. **Trigger:** `what should I do`
2. **System:** "Energy? (high/medium/low)" → You say `high`.
3. **Result:** System surfaces items tagged `#deep`, `#focus`, or `#2hours`.
   - _"High energy. Try: 'Write Q3 Strategy Doc' (#deep) - Flagged"_
4. **Action:** Select it. System flags it as the *only* thing to look at.

**Why it works:** Matches your mental state to the task difficulty, preventing procrastination on hard tasks.

---

## 6. The "Context Switch" (Errands)
**Situation:** You're leaving the house to go to the store.

**Recipe:**
1. **Trigger:** `show me @errands` or `leaving the house`
2. **System:** Shows only items tagged `@errands` or `@hardware_store`.
   - _"While you're out: 'Buy lightbulbs', 'Post letter', 'Pick up milk'"_
3. **Action:** Complete them in one batch trip.

**Why it works:** Batching by location is the classic GTD efficiency hack.

---

## 7. The "I'm Stuck" Unblocker
**Situation:** You're staring at a task "Write Report" and procrastinating.

**Recipe:**
1. **Trigger:** `I'm stuck`
2. **System:** "What's blocking you? (Unclear step / Waiting on info / Too big / Dreading it)"
3. **You:** "Too big"
4. **System:** "Let's break it down. What's the *very first* tiny step?"
   - **You:** "Open a blank document"
   - **System:** "Added 'Open blank document' to Next Actions. Do it now?"

**Why it works:** Overcomes inertia by lowering the barrier to entry to the absolute floor.
